Modeling of Boxes and Plenums

 

The terminology of a “ box” and a “plenum” is both used to describe a container of a fixed volume that is connected to one or more pipes in the intake system. The user has to specify the box volume in cm3 and the pipe end configuration at the entry or exit from the box. The user has a choice between a sharp edge and a rounded edge for the pipe end.

 An inlet box can be used to simulate an air cleaner box, a resonating chamber (called a boost bottle) or a collector with a large volume (typically the cavity under the carburetor of a V-engine intake manifold to which the cylinder pipes connect).
